- **Duke dominance**: Three of four players claimed Duke for Tax on their first turns. The Duke's +3 coin advantage is so powerful that claiming it (truthfully or not) is nearly mandatory. No player ever successfully challenged a Duke claim in this game.
- **Gemini as kingmaker-in-reverse**: Gemini's early elimination removed a potential challenger/blocker from the game, simplifying the dynamics. Gemini's failed challenges actually strengthened Claude (who proved Assassin) and ChatGPT (who proved Duke).
- **Income trap**: ChatGPT fell into the "Income trap" in the endgame, collecting only +1 per turn while Claude collected +3 via Tax. Over turns 11-28, this compounding advantage made the outcome nearly inevitable.
- **Challenge reluctance**: All players showed extreme reluctance to challenge established claims. Once a player had claimed a role two or three times, opponents treated it as confirmed even without proof. This benefited Claude, who may have been bluffing the Contessa block on Turn 21.
- **Assassination economics**: The assassination mechanic created interesting dynamics. Claude's Turn 5 assassination cost 3 coins but was blocked after proving the Assassin -- a net loss. ChatGPT's Turn 21 assassination cost 3 coins and was also blocked -- a pure loss. In both cases, the 3-coin investment yielded nothing, making Coup (at 7 coins) the more reliable elimination method.
