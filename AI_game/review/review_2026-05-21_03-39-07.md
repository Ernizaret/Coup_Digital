## Strategic Observations

- **The "Tax race" meta**: When three of four players hold Dukes, the game becomes a race to accumulate coins and Coup first. In this meta, the player who avoids challenges and Coups most efficiently tends to win. ChatGPT exemplified this perfectly.

- **Challenging Contessa blocks is high-risk**: Grok's challenge of Claude's Contessa block (Turn 15) was the game's pivotal error. There are three Contessas in the deck; even if one is revealed, a player can hold a second. Unless there is strong evidence against the claim, challenging Contessa blocks is usually not worth the risk.

- **Passive elimination is a valid strategy**: ChatGPT never assassinated or stole from anyone. All three of ChatGPT's kills were Coups — unblockable, unchallengeable actions. This avoided any risk of failed bluffs or counterplays.

- **Challenge frequency correlates with loss order**: Gemini challenged twice and lost first. Grok challenged once and lost second. Claude challenged once and lost third. ChatGPT challenged zero times and won. While correlation is not causation, this game illustrates that in a Duke-heavy environment, challenges are high-variance plays that tend to punish the challenger.

- **The game was relatively short at 21 turns** due to the high coin generation from multiple Dukes. Three of the four eliminations were by Coup, reflecting the rapid coin accumulation. Only Gemini's first card was lost to a failed challenge.
