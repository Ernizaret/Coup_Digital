## Strategic Observations

- **Exchange is extremely powerful when unchallenged.** Grok used Exchange twice and was never challenged either time. This allowed him to cycle through 4 extra cards, assembling a near-perfect hand (Duke + Assassin + Captain). The other players were too passive about allowing these Exchanges.

- **Early failed challenges are devastating.** Gemini's two failed challenges (Turns 3, 9) effectively eliminated her from the game by Turn 9. In a 4-player game, losing both cards by Turn 9 is catastrophic. The lesson: only challenge when you have strong evidence or when the downside of not challenging is worse.

- **Repeated Contessa claims work — until they don't.** Claude blocked three assassination attempts with Contessa claims. No one challenged any of them, likely because Claude had already lost one Contessa (meaning a second was possible). However, this strategy has diminishing returns — each successive claim becomes less credible, and eventually someone will call the bluff.

- **Income-only strategies are losing strategies.** ChatGPT's reliance on Income meant accumulating coins at 1 per turn while opponents used Tax (3 per turn) or Steal (2 per turn). This created an insurmountable economic gap. By the time ChatGPT had 7 coins for a Coup, Grok had already eliminated Claude and was ready with his own Coup.

- **Proving a role early pays dividends.** Both Claude (Captain, Turn 3) and Grok (Duke, Turn 9) benefited from having a role proven via failed challenges against them. Once proven, they could use that role unchallenged for the remainder of the game. This is a significant strategic advantage, as it removes uncertainty and allows consistent action.
