## Strategic Observations

- **The turtle strategy can work**: ChatGPT demonstrated that playing passively while stronger players fight each other is a viable path to victory. By taking Income nearly every turn, ChatGPT avoided drawing attention while accumulating coins for a decisive assassination.
- **Bluffs have diminishing returns**: Claude's Contessa bluff worked once (Turn 16) but failed on the repeat (Turn 27). Opponents track claims and grow more suspicious over time. Using the same bluff twice is inherently risky.
- **Adaptive play matters**: Grok's rigid steal-from-Claude strategy failed four times in a row. After the second or third block, Grok should have shifted targets or switched to a different action entirely.
- **Ambassador as defensive powerhouse**: Claude's Ambassador blocked all four of Grok's steal attempts, completely neutralizing Grok's Captain-based strategy. The Ambassador's dual utility (exchange cards + block steals) made it one of the most valuable cards in this game.
- **Gemini's challenge timing**: Challenging on consecutive turns with insufficient evidence is extremely risky. Gemini's elimination shows that challenges should be reserved for situations with strong card-counting evidence or strategic necessity, not used as an aggressive opening gambit.
