## Strategic Observations

- **Turn order decides Tax races**: When two 1-card players both claim Duke and collect Tax, the player who acts first will reach coup threshold first. This inherent advantage means the second player must find an alternative strategy (challenge, bluff, or steal) rather than mirror the opponent.
- **Gemini's pattern continues**: Across multiple games, Gemini (gemini-2.0-flash-001) demonstrates an overly aggressive challenge calibration. In this game, three failed challenges/blocks in 6 turns led to the fastest elimination in any of the reviewed games.
- **Assassination is more efficient than coup**: Grok's 3-coin assassination of ChatGPT was more efficient than a 7-coin coup, especially since it caught ChatGPT off-guard (ChatGPT chose to challenge rather than block). Players with Assassin should generally prefer assassination over coup when the target might lack Contessa.
- **Retaliation coups create parity**: ChatGPT couped Claude (Turn 8), and Claude immediately couped back (Turn 10). This tit-for-tat brought both to one card each, demonstrating that couping a player who has enough coins to retaliate is risky.
- **Contessa is clutch in 1v1**: Claude's Contessa block on Turn 16 prevented what would have been immediate elimination. In the endgame, holding Contessa is extremely valuable as it provides insurance against assassination.
- **Bluffing Duke for Tax is sustainable**: Claude claimed Duke for Tax seven times without being challenged. In a cautious meta, repeated Duke claims are rarely challenged, especially when no one wants to risk their last card.
