Grok's victory was built on a foundation of strong cards, zero bluffing, and excellent memory of revealed cards.

## Strategic Observations

- **Block stalemates define the mid-game**: When multiple players have blocking cards (Ambassador, Captain, Contessa), the mid-game devolves into repeated steal/assassinate attempts that are blocked, with players falling back to Income. Breaking these stalemates requires successful challenges.
- **Claude's analytical challenge skill is the strongest across all players**: Claude is the only player who successfully challenged a block in this game (ChatGPT's Contessa, turn 17), using deductive reasoning about claimed cards. This skill is Claude's greatest asset but also their greatest risk, as the turn 19 challenge of Grok's Captain backfired.
- **Bluffing Contessa is high-risk, high-reward**: Claude's Contessa bluff on turn 20 worked and saved their life for two more turns. ChatGPT's Contessa bluff on turn 8 also worked but was eventually caught on turn 17. Contessa bluffs succeed because challenging them risks losing a card to a legitimate claim.
- **Grok's Duke+Captain combination won two of five games**: This card combination appeared to be the strongest in the meta, providing both income generation and steal/block capability without any need to bluff.
- **Gemini's persistent failure pattern**: Across all five games, Gemini challenged early and incorrectly every single time, always being the first player eliminated. The Gemini model appears to have a fundamental miscalibration of challenge probability, consistently overestimating the likelihood that opponents are bluffing.
- **Income as a default action**: ChatGPT took Income on 5 of their 8 turns. While safe, this pace of coin accumulation (1 per turn) is far too slow to threaten opponents. ChatGPT never reached 7 coins for a coup, making their elimination inevitable.
- **Information tracking wins games**: The two most decisive plays in this game (Claude challenging ChatGPT's Contessa on turn 17, Grok challenging Claude's Captain on turn 22) were both based on tracking which cards had been revealed and which claims were inconsistent. Memory and deduction are the core skills that separate winners from losers in Coup.
