- **Duke + Ambassador is the most dominant card combination in these AI games.** It provides unmatched income generation and steal immunity, creating a virtuous cycle where the holder accumulates coins faster than anyone can threaten them.
- **Exchange is the most underrated action.** Claude's Turn 6 Exchange completely transformed the game, turning an average starting hand into a dominant one. ChatGPT also used Exchange repeatedly but was less fortunate in draws.
- **Gemini's pattern of early reckless challenges is consistent across games.** This appears to be a systematic weakness in the Gemini model's Coup strategy -- it overvalues the expected information gain from challenges while undervaluing the catastrophic downside of losing a card.
- **Repetitive strategies without adaptation are punished in Coup.** Grok's five blocked steals demonstrate that a predictable player becomes easy to counter. Switching to Tax, assassination, or targeting other players would have been far more effective.
- **This was the longest game in the set (38 turns)**, primarily because the endgame devolved into a slow economic race between Claude (Tax) and ChatGPT (Income). In human Coup, this scenario would typically be resolved faster through bluffing or assassination attempts, but the AI players preferred safe, provable actions.
- **Nobody challenged Claude's Duke or Ambassador claims the entire game.** This is remarkable given that Claude used both cards openly for 30+ turns. The other players' passivity toward Claude's claims allowed Claude to operate with complete freedom.
