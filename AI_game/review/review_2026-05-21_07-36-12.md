## Strategic Observations

- **Assassin + Captain is the strongest opening combination.** Grok's hand allowed cheap eliminations (Assassinate costs 3, far less than Coup's 7) and coin denial through stealing. This pairing enables early aggression that passive hands cannot counter.
- **Bluffing a Contessa block is extremely risky.** ChatGPT's false Contessa claim on Turn 8 backfired, costing two cards instead of one. When assassination is not blockable, accepting the loss of one card is often the better play.
- **Gemini's challenge reasoning is consistently flawed.** Gemini cited "Grok has no coins" as justification for challenges, but this was factually wrong. Poor information tracking compounds Gemini's already aggressive challenge tendencies.
- **1v1 endgames with matched roles become pure economic races.** Once Claude and Grok both held a Duke and one card each, the game devolved into a coin-accumulation race. Whoever had more coins (or fewer coins to spend on the coup threshold) would win. Grok's slight coin lead from earlier plays was decisive.
- **Aggressive early play sets the trajectory.** Grok's two eliminations by Turn 11 meant Grok faced only one opponent with a built-in advantage. Conservative players like Claude, who took income and exchanged early, found themselves reacting to Grok's tempo rather than setting their own.
