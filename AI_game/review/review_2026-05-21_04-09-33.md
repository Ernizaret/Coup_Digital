- **Claude's four-turn Duke bluff with double Contessa is the most audacious play in the entire game set.** It demonstrates that in Coup, confident repetition of a claim builds credibility. Nobody challenged because Claude had been "right" every previous time. The bluff only failed when Claude was on one card and the risk/reward for the challenger shifted.

This is the correct strategic approach to challenging in Coup.
- **The mutual coup exchange between Claude and ChatGPT (Turns 8-9) was a classic "two wolves fighting while the fox watches" scenario.** Grok benefited enormously from staying out of the conflict. This is a recurring pattern in multiplayer Coup -- the players who fight each other tend to lose to the player who avoids combat.

The model needs significant recalibration of its challenge-probability assessment.
- **Exchange remains a powerful early-game action** -- Grok's Turn 3 Exchange set up the entire winning strategy by allowing hand optimization. The ability to see 4 cards and keep the best 2 is a significant advantage that compounds over the course of the game.
- **This was the second-shortest game (13 turns)**, reflecting the speed at which players were eliminated through challenges and coups. The aggressive play style of Claude (bluffing) and ChatGPT (retaliatory coups) compressed the game, leaving Grok to clean up efficiently.
