## Strategic Observations

- **Early Exchanges can be game-defining**: Claude's turn 2 Exchange shaped their entire winning strategy. By deliberately choosing Captain (and likely Contessa), Claude built a hand optimized for the steal-heavy game that followed.
- **Gemini's challenge pattern was self-destructive**: Challenging Tax claims in a game where multiple players may legitimately have Duke is extremely risky. Gemini challenged twice and lost twice, achieving nothing but their own elimination.
- **Ambassador as a persistent blocker**: ChatGPT used Ambassador to block steals three consecutive times (turns 9, 11, 12). This demonstrated the Ambassador's defensive value, though it also created suspicion that eventually led to a challenge.
- **Assassination is the strongest offensive action**: Grok's assassination of ChatGPT on turn 14 was a two-for-one play: the failed challenge cost one card, and the assassination itself cost another. This is the highest-value play possible in Coup.
- **Captain dominates the endgame**: In the 1v1 between Claude (Captain) and Grok (Duke), the Captain's ability to both steal and block steals made it strictly superior. Duke's Tax (+3) could not keep pace with Captain's Steal (+2 for you, -2 for them = net 4-coin swing).
- **Passive play loses**: ChatGPT and Gemini both played passively (Income, Tax) and were eliminated. Claude and Grok, who used Steal and Assassinate, were the last two standing.
