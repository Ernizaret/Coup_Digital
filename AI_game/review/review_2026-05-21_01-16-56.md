## Strategic Observations

- **Assassin is the great equalizer**: ChatGPT demonstrated that a patient Assassin strategy can defeat a dominant Duke/Captain hand. While Grok taxed for 3 coins per turn, ChatGPT needed only 3 coins total per assassination. The Assassin provides the best coin-to-impact ratio in the game.

- **Card replacement creates information uncertainty**: Claude's failed challenge in turn 8 highlights a critical strategic concept -- when a player reveals a card during a challenge, they receive a replacement card from the deck. This means a player who has revealed two different cards over the course of the game may have any card as their second, not necessarily one of the two previously revealed. Players must track not just what was revealed, but when cards were replaced.

- **Patience can be a winning strategy**: ChatGPT's 14-turn Income run looked weak but was actually optimal. Not every hand requires aggressive play. Recognizing when to wait and when to strike is a high-level skill.

- **Gemini's pattern is systemic**: Across three games now, Gemini has been eliminated early through reckless challenges. This appears to be a fundamental issue with the model's decision-making in Coup, not a one-time mistake. Gemini consistently overestimates the value of challenging and underestimates the cost of failure.

- **Contessa absence decides endgames**: The final 1v1 between Grok and ChatGPT was decided by Grok's lack of a Contessa. Against assassination, the Contessa is the only defense. Without it, a player with 3+ coins and an Assassin has an unblockable path to eliminating an influence.
