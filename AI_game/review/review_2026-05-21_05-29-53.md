## Strategic Observations

- **The Duke mirror**: This game featured three players (Claude, Grok, and arguably ChatGPT post-Exchange) all using Tax as their primary income source. When multiple players have Duke access, the game becomes about who can accumulate and spend coins most efficiently. Claude and Grok were evenly matched, but Claude's slight turn-order advantage in the 1v1 proved decisive.
- **Exchange value**: ChatGPT's Turn 2 Exchange was one of the few strategic card-management plays across all games. By returning Contessa and Assassin and keeping Ambassador + something else, ChatGPT attempted to optimize their hand. However, the value was undermined by passive play afterward.
- **Gemini's pattern**: Across multiple games, Gemini consistently makes early challenges that fail, leading to rapid elimination. This represents a systematic strategic weakness -- Gemini appears to challenge too aggressively without sufficient evidence.
- **1v1 dynamics**: When two Duke players reach a 1v1, the game becomes largely deterministic -- whoever has the turn advantage and enough coins to coup first wins. There is limited room for outplay when both players are collecting +3 per turn.
- **Conservative endgame**: Claude's failed challenge of Grok on Turn 22 is a rare example of aggression in the late game. Generally, when both players are on one card, challenges become extremely risky since the cost of failure is elimination. Claude survived only because the lost card was the less critical one.
