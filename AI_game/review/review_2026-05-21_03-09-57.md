## Strategic Observations

- **Passive play can win**: ChatGPT demonstrated that in a four-player game, staying out of conflict and letting aggressive players weaken each other is a viable path to victory. They won despite never successfully completing an offensive action.

- **Failed challenges are devastating**: Gemini's two failed challenges were the fastest elimination in the game. The penalty for a wrong challenge (losing influence) is steep enough that challenges should only be made with high confidence.

- **Proven claims create lasting protection**: Once Claude revealed their Assassin on Turn 5, it became a proven threat that other players respected (except Grok, who challenged it again and was eliminated). Similarly, Claude's consistent Duke claim was never challenged because it had been implicitly validated by consistency.

- **Card tracking errors cost games**: Both Claude (Turn 10, misjudging Grok's hand) and Grok (Turn 11, challenging a proven Assassin) made card-tracking mistakes that directly cost them the game. Accurate mental tracking of revealed and claimed cards is essential.

- **The turtle strategy has a ceiling**: While ChatGPT won this game, their Income-heavy approach only worked because other players fought. In a more strategic field, a player taking only Income would fall behind in coins compared to Tax claimers and eventually be couped.
