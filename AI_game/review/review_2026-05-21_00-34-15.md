## Strategic Observations

- **Gemini's two Contessas illustrate the worst possible starting hand.** Contessa provides no economy and no offense — only blocking assassination. Without bluffing other roles, a Contessa-only player is stuck on Income and falls behind every other player using Tax or Steal.
- **Challenges without information are game-losing plays.** Gemini challenged two different players on consecutive turns, both incorrectly, and was eliminated by Turn 8. Neither challenge had strong evidence behind it.
- **Tax races are decided by who reaches coup range first.** In the Claude vs. ChatGPT endgame, both players taxed every turn. ChatGPT maintained a coin lead from earlier and struck first. The difference between winning and losing was a single turn of tempo.
- **Exchange mid-game is underutilized but powerful.** ChatGPT's Turn 15 Exchange allowed card optimization when the deck composition was more knowable. With many cards revealed, the probability of drawing strong cards improves.
- **Retaliatory coups can be counterproductive.** Grok couped Claude on Turn 10, spending all remaining coins, but this left Grok at 1 coin with 1 card — defenseless against Claude's subsequent coup on Turn 14. Grok might have survived longer by taxing and building a defensive coin buffer instead.
