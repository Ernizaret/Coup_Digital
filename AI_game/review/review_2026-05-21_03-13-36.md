## Strategic Observations

- **Hand composition matters enormously**: Claude's two-Contessa hand was essentially unplayable without successful bluffs. Having at least one economic card (Duke) or offensive card (Assassin, Captain) is critical for viable gameplay.

- **Exchange can be game-changing**: Grok's Turn 2 Exchange allowed them to reshape their hand, shedding an Assassin and Ambassador. Ironically, Grok later had an Assassin again (possibly drawn back or kept one), suggesting the Exchange was used to optimize the second card. The ability to see four cards and choose two is a powerful strategic tool.

- **The Assassin is the strongest endgame card**: In the 1v1, Grok's Assassin eliminated both of ChatGPT's cards in a single 3-coin action. Compared to the 7-coin Coup (which only removes one card), the Assassin is dramatically more efficient when the opponent cannot block it.

- **Challenging an Assassin before a block is available is suicidal**: ChatGPT's Turn 21 challenge of Grok's Assassin was the game-losing move. By challenging first and losing their Contessa, they removed their own ability to block the assassination. The correct play would have been to decline the challenge and attempt a Contessa block (whether real or bluffed).

- **Income-only strategies have a hard ceiling**: Both Claude and Gemini were forced into Income-only patterns and both were eliminated. Without the ability to gain 3 coins per turn (Tax) or steal from opponents, a player cannot keep pace with Duke-wielding opponents and will eventually be couped.
