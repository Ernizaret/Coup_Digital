## Strategic Observations

- **Passive play can win**: Grok demonstrated that an extremely passive Income-based strategy can survive to the endgame if other players fight each other. The key is making one decisive move at the right moment.
- **Reckless challenging is deadly**: Gemini's elimination by Turn 7 from two failed challenges is a textbook example of how aggressive challenging without strong information backfires.
- **Blocking Foreign Aid with Duke is powerful**: Claude used this twice to slow Grok's economy, which was one of the strongest positional plays in the game.
- **Card cycling is tempo-negative**: ChatGPT's repeated Exchange actions meant they were spending turns not gaining coins, while Claude and Grok built economic advantages.
- **The Contessa bluff is high-risk**: Claude's final Contessa bluff was the right conceptual play (survive assassination), but it was correctly read by Grok, showing that bluffs under pressure in the endgame are more likely to be challenged.
- **Claude's Duke-based economy was dominant**: Claude collected Tax six times unchallenged, amassing enough coins for two Coups. The Duke remains the strongest economic card.
