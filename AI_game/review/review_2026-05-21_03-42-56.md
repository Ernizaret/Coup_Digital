## Strategic Observations

- **Gemini's challenge pattern is a consistent liability**: Across multiple games, Gemini (google/gemini-2.0-flash-001) demonstrates an overly aggressive challenge instinct that leads to early elimination. In this game, two failed challenges in 5 turns. In the previous game, two failed challenges also led to early elimination. The model appears to have a low threshold for challenging, which is exploitable.

- **The "Tax-and-Coup" strategy dominates Duke-heavy metas**: When multiple players hold Dukes, the optimal strategy appears to be: Tax every turn, avoid all confrontation, Coup when forced or when you can eliminate a threat. ChatGPT executed this perfectly across two consecutive wins.

- **Turn order is decisive in endgame Coups**: In the final three-player phase, the player who Coups first must choose between two opponents. This choice is often the difference between winning and losing. Claude chose to eliminate the weaker opponent (Grok) instead of the next-to-act opponent (ChatGPT), which proved fatal.

- **Captain is a poor endgame card**: Grok's endgame as a solo Captain demonstrated the card's limitations. Steal only works if the target has coins and does not block. In a one-card endgame, the target almost always blocks (since losing a challenge means elimination), making Steal unreliable. Captain provides no income generation on its own, unlike Duke (Tax).

- **Bluffing Captain blocks is highly effective**: Claude successfully blocked two Steals by claiming Captain (Turns 14, 17) despite likely not having one (Claude's remaining card after losing Contessa and proving Duke was ambiguous). No one challenged these blocks because challenging a one-card player's block risks nothing for the blocker (they lose the same card either way) but costs the challenger a card if wrong.
