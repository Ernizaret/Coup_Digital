## Strategic Observations

- **Claim tracking wins games**: Grok's victory was built on tracking which cards each player claimed. When ChatGPT claimed Contessa, Duke, and Captain, Grok correctly identified this as impossible and challenged. This is the highest-level play in Coup.

- **Bluffing blocks is high-risk**: Both ChatGPT (Captain block, turn 6) and Claude (Captain block, turn 10) lost cards by bluffing blocks. Blocking claims are particularly vulnerable to challenge because opponents are highly motivated to verify them -- a successful block denies them the action's benefit.

- **Proven cards create safe income**: Both Claude and Grok used proven Dukes for reliable Tax collection. Once a card is revealed and reshuffled, opponents generally stop challenging that claim.

- **Fast games favor the strong hand**: At only 10 turns, this was the shortest game in the set. The speed was driven by Gemini's self-destruction (2 turns), ChatGPT's overextension (2 more turns), and Grok's efficient aggression. When weaker players eliminate themselves quickly, the game collapses to a 1v1 decided by card quality and information advantage.

- **Gemini's flawed heuristic**: Gemini's reasoning that "only has 2 coins, so they can't be telling the truth" suggests the model confused game mechanics. This is the type of fundamental rules misunderstanding that would need to be addressed in the AI's training or prompt.
