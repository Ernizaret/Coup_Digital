## Strategic Observations

- **Gemini's persistent challenge behavior is a severe liability**: Across two games now, Gemini has lost all cards to failed challenges. Gemini appears to challenge too aggressively, particularly against Duke claims, which are almost always legitimate in these AI games.

- **The steal-block cycle is a trap**: Grok attempted 5 steals and was blocked on all 5. When opponents hold Captain or Ambassador, repeated steal attempts waste turns without advancing the game state. Grok should have pivoted to Tax (claiming Duke), Income, or Foreign Aid after the first few blocks.

- **Duke mirror matches devolve into coin-counting**: When two surviving players both hold Duke, the endgame becomes a mechanical exercise: Tax, Tax, Coup, Tax, Tax, Coup. The player with the coin lead or turn order advantage wins. There is no bluffing or deception involved.

- **Proven cards eliminate the bluffing dimension**: Once ChatGPT's Duke was proven via Gemini's failed challenge, the entire bluffing element was removed for that card. Other players stopped challenging, and ChatGPT could Tax freely. This suggests that proving a card early is a significant strategic advantage, even though it happens involuntarily via opponent challenges.

- **Turn order matters in the endgame**: ChatGPT won partly because of having the right turn order in the final coin race. In Coup, going second in a head-to-head gives a slight advantage because you can react to your opponent's last action.
