## Strategic Observations

- **The passive strategy wins when opponents are aggressive**: For the second consecutive game, the most passive player (ChatGPT) won by letting others eliminate each other. This suggests the AI models that play aggressively (Grok, Claude) are systematically disadvantaged in multi-player games because they attract attention and spend resources fighting.

- **Gemini's challenge addiction is a consistent weakness**: Across multiple games, Gemini consistently challenges other players' claims and consistently loses, often against proven cards. This pattern suggests a systematic flaw in Gemini's threat assessment -- it challenges too aggressively without properly weighing revealed information.

- **Ambassador as the best defensive card**: Grok's Ambassador blocked two steal attempts (Turns 4 and 12), survived two challenges, and was never lost. Combined with Duke for income, Ambassador + Duke appears to be the strongest card combination in the game.

- **Mutual destruction benefits the bystander**: The Claude-Grok rivalry (steals, coups, counter-coups) consumed both players' resources and ultimately eliminated both, handing the game to the uninvolved ChatGPT. In multiplayer Coup, feuds between two players almost always benefit the third.

- **Income accumulation is viable but slow**: ChatGPT took 8 Income actions over 19 turns to reach coup range. While this worked due to opponents self-destructing, a more aware opponent table would have targeted the passive player before they could accumulate enough for a coup.
