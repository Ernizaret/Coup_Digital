## Strategic Observations

- **Duplicate starting cards are a significant disadvantage.** Gemini's two Captains meant no bluffing diversity and no strong economic action. This hand essentially doomed Gemini from the start.
- **Card counting is the endgame skill.** Both the final play (Grok catching Claude's Duke bluff) and the general tracking of revealed cards proved decisive. In a 4-player game with 15 cards total, by the midgame enough information exists to make informed challenges.
- **Assassination is high-risk when opponents have Contessa.** Claude's Turn 10 assassination attempt cost 3 coins and a card (from the failed block challenge), with no result. Assassination is most effective when you can verify the target lacks a Contessa.
- **Tax is the dominant economic strategy.** Grok's four successful Tax claims (12 coins) dwarfed the income from other players' cautious approaches. When Tax claims go unchallenged, the Duke holder snowballs.
- **Challenging the wrong things is worse than not challenging.** Gemini challenged three times, succeeded once (and it helped an opponent), and lost two cards. Grok challenged once unwisely (Turn 11) but made up for it with one perfectly timed challenge (Turn 15). Quality over quantity in challenges is essential.
