## Strategic Observations

- **Claude's card-counting ability is a dominant strategy**: Across Games 4 and 5, Claude has made three card-counting challenges, all successful. This skill -- tracking revealed cards and determining when a claim is mathematically impossible -- is the single most effective strategy observed in these games. No other AI consistently applies this technique.

- **The steal loop is nearly unbeatable in the endgame**: In both Games 4 and 5, Claude won the endgame by repeatedly stealing from the last opponent. The steal loop creates a mathematical impossibility for the victim: gaining +1 from Income but losing -2 to steals means a net -1 per cycle. The only counter is blocking, but if the victim has no Captain or Ambassador, they lose. And if they bluff one, they risk losing their last card to a challenge.

- **ChatGPT's failure to block steals is a systematic weakness**: In both Games 4 and 5, ChatGPT passively accepted repeated steals rather than attempting blocks. This passive behavior allowed Claude to dominate unopposed. Even a bluffed block has positive expected value when the alternative is certain coin loss, especially in the early steal iterations when the opponent may not be confident enough to challenge.

- **Gemini's AI is fundamentally broken**: Five games, five first-place eliminations. Gemini has never survived past Turn 11, and in three of five games was eliminated by Turn 6 or earlier. Gemini's problems are twofold: (1) excessively aggressive challenges against legitimate claims, and (2) transparently poor bluffs that are easily caught. Gemini provides no competitive pressure and effectively starts every game as a 3-player game.

- **Bluffing Assassin when Assassins are visible is a known trap**: Both Grok (Game 4, Turn 16) and Grok (Game 5, Turn 16 -- same turn number) bluffed Assassin when enough Assassins were revealed for card counting to disprove the claim. This suggests that bluffing Assassin is particularly risky because Assassin losses from failed challenges and assassinations create visible information that enables card counting.

- **The meta across all five games**: ChatGPT won 3 of 5 games, Claude won 2 of 5. Gemini was eliminated first in all 5. The Duke is the most powerful card, enabling both income (Tax) and denial (blocking Foreign Aid). Captain is the best endgame card for steal loops. Contessa provides essential assassination defense. Ambassador is the weakest card, only useful for blocking steals. The most effective overall strategy is: hold Duke for income, survive the mid-game, then use Coup or steals to eliminate remaining opponents.
