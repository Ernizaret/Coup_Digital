## Strategic Observations

- **Early hand optimization is powerful**: Grok's Turn 4 Exchange -- returning two Ambassadors for better cards -- was the most impactful single play in the game. It established the Duke access that funded the entire winning strategy.
- **Gemini's challenge pattern is a consistent weakness**: Across multiple games, Gemini challenges far too aggressively. In this game, 3 challenges in 7 turns (2 failures) led to rapid elimination. Gemini needs a higher threshold for challenging.
- **The Duke economic gap is decisive**: In the Grok vs. ChatGPT endgame, both had Dukes initially, but after ChatGPT lost one card and could not safely claim Duke (Grok might challenge), the 3:1 income ratio made the outcome deterministic.
- **Re-bluffing a just-caught card is high-risk**: Claude bluffed Duke on Turn 6 right after losing the real Duke on Turn 2. Opponents are more alert to repeated claims of the same card, especially after a reveal.
- **This was the shortest game of the batch at 19 turns**: Fast elimination of Gemini (Turn 7) and Claude (Turn 10) led to a quick two-player endgame. When two players are eliminated early, the remaining contest is often a coin-race decided by card quality.
