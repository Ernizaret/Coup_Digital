## Strategic Observations

- **The Tax-and-block strategy is dominant in long games.** Claude's combination of Duke (Tax) and Captain (block steals) created an impenetrable economic engine. When no one challenges either claim, this generates 3 coins per turn while denying opponents any way to slow it down.
- **One-dimensional aggression fails against prepared defense.** Grok's 10+ steal attempts against Claude all failed to the same Captain block. At no point did Grok consider challenging the block, targeting a different player, or switching to a different action. This inflexibility was the primary reason Grok lost.
- **The game became extremely long (40 turns) due to the stalemate.** The Grok-Claude steal/block cycle from Turns 25-39 added 15 turns of no meaningful progress. A smarter Grok would have challenged the Captain block or switched strategies much earlier.
- **Gemini's reckless challenging was self-destructive.** Across all these games, Gemini consistently challenges too early and too often, losing cards unnecessarily. This pattern of premature challenges is Gemini's most consistent weakness.
- **Card counting errors can be costly but not always fatal.** Claude's Turn 24 Duke challenge was based on incorrect card counting (miscounting the number of Dukes in the deck), but Claude had enough of a lead to survive the mistake.
