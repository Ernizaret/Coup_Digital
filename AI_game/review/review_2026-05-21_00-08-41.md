Grok's initial hand (Captain + Ambassador) was solid, and after losing the Ambassador to Claude's assassination, Grok cycled through an impressive array of cards: the Assassin used to eliminate Claude, the Duke that blocked ChatGPT's Foreign Aid, and finally the Contessa that blocked ChatGPT's assassination attempt.

The key to Grok's victory was the card reshuffling mechanic. Every time Grok revealed a card for a successful challenge defense, that card was shuffled back and replaced. This meant Grok effectively got to "refresh" their hand multiple times, drawing into exactly what they needed at each critical moment. Grok revealed four different card types across the game (Captain, Assassin, Duke, Contessa), demonstrating either extraordinary luck or the statistical inevitability of cycling through the 15-card deck.

When both remaining opponents launched assassination attempts in the final turns, Grok simply blocked with a real Contessa and let them self-destruct.

- **Card cycling is a powerful mechanic.** Grok revealed four different card types across the game thanks to the reshuffle-on-successful-defense rule. Players who challenge and fail not only lose a card but give the defender a fresh draw, potentially improving their hand. This game demonstrated how dangerous it is to challenge a player who keeps winning challenges.
- **Claude's bluffing was sophisticated but ultimately unsustainable.** Claude successfully bluffed Duke (twice), Ambassador (once), and only failed on the Contessa bluff. A 3-for-4 bluff success rate is impressive, but the one failure was fatal.
- **Gemini served as the "challenge police" to mixed results.** Gemini challenged 4 times across the game, succeeding only once (Claude's Contessa). However, that one success eliminated Claude, so Gemini's challenge-heavy strategy had significant impact even though it was ultimately self-destructive.
- **The endgame was decided by Contessa holdings.** The final two turns both involved assassination attempts blocked by real Contessas. Both attackers challenged the blocks and lost. This highlights the Contessa's enormous endgame value -- in late-game 1v1 or 1v1v1 situations, having a real Contessa is often decisive.
- **Income was underused as a strategy.** Most players defaulted to Income when they had no better option, but no one used it as a deliberate strategy. The game's economy was driven by Tax and Steal, with Income as a fallback.
