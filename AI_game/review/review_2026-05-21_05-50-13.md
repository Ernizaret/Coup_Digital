## Strategic Observations

- **Tax as a dominant strategy**: Grok's Tax-heavy approach generated massive coin advantage. Collecting +3 per turn with no challenge is far superior to Income's +1, and nobody successfully contested Grok's Duke claim across the entire 29-turn game.
- **Failed challenges as game-enders**: Both Gemini's elimination and Claude's final loss came from failed challenges. Challenging is high-risk/high-reward, and in this game every challenge against a truthful claim was catastrophic.
- **Captain-block stalemate**: The Captain vs. Captain dynamic between Grok and Claude created a theft stalemate in the early-mid game, forcing Claude to seek other targets.
- **Card counting errors are fatal**: Claude's endgame challenge was based on faulty math about the number of Dukes in the game. Accurate card counting is essential in late-game Coup, and a single miscalculation can be immediately fatal.
- **Gemini's passive-aggressive contradiction**: Gemini played Income exclusively for actions but then aggressively challenged others twice in a row. This inconsistency -- passive in their own turns, recklessly aggressive in response to others -- led to the fastest elimination in the game.
