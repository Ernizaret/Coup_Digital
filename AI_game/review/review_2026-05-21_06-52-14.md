## Strategic Observations

- **Card counting wins games.** Claude's decisive victory came from tracking revealed cards. The final play, catching ChatGPT's impossible Contessa claim, was pure deduction based on counting the two revealed Contessas. This is the highest-level play in the transcript.
- **Proving a Duke early creates a snowball effect.** Once Claude's Duke was proven twice, no one challenged subsequent Tax claims. This gave Claude a free 3 coins every turn, making Claude the economic leader throughout the game.
- **Repeated failed challenges are catastrophic.** Gemini's two failed challenges against the same proven card effectively ended Gemini's game by Turn 11. Players must update their mental model after a challenge fails.
- **Exchange without exploitation is wasted tempo.** Both Grok and ChatGPT exchanged cards multiple times without translating their new hands into aggressive plays. Exchange costs a turn and only pays off if it enables stronger subsequent actions.
- **Bluffing blocks is dangerous in the late game.** ChatGPT's Contessa bluff on Turn 27 and Claude's Captain bluff on Turn 13 illustrate opposite outcomes. In the late game, when more cards are revealed, bluffs become easier to catch.
