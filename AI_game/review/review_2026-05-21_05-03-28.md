## Strategic Observations

- **Card counting errors are fatal**: Both Claude (Turn 17) and Grok (Turn 22) lost to failed challenges where they believed all Dukes were accounted for. Card exchanges make tracking impossible, and overconfidence in card counting is a recurring losing pattern.
- **Exchange reshuffles the information game**: Grok's exchange on Turn 19 allowed them to swap into a Duke that Claude thought was impossible, invalidating Claude's careful card tracking. Exchange actions significantly degrade opponents' information quality.
- **Targeting the right opponent matters**: Grok's coup on Claude (Turn 14) instead of ChatGPT was a pivotal error. Always consider which opponent benefits most from surviving.
- **Gemini's challenge addiction**: Across all these games, Gemini consistently challenges too aggressively and too early, losing cards at an unsustainable rate.
- **The Duke is king**: Every player who held a Duke and kept it survived longer. Tax providing 3 coins per turn remains the strongest single-card advantage in the game.
