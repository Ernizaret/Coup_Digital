## Player Breakdowns

### ChatGPT
ChatGPT had the most active and complex game. With a Duke providing consistent Tax income and an Ambassador for blocking steals, ChatGPT accumulated coins rapidly and was the first to coup (Turn 9, removing Grok's Duke). However, ChatGPT's downfall was excessive bluffing. They claimed at least four distinct roles across the game (Duke, Ambassador, Contessa, Captain), and Claude caught them twice: once on the Captain block (Turn 16) and once on the Ambassador block (Turn 20). The second failed bluff eliminated ChatGPT entirely. ChatGPT's strategic error was relying on the same opponent (Claude) not tracking their cumulative role claims.

### Grok
Grok played a survivalist game. After an early steal attempt was blocked (Turn 2) and their Duke was couped away (Turn 9), Grok spent the mid-game conserving resources with Income. Grok successfully stole from ChatGPT on Turn 16 (after Claude's challenge broke ChatGPT's block) and attempted an assassination on ChatGPT on Turn 10 (blocked by Contessa). The winning assassination on Turn 21 against Claude was clean and decisive -- Grok had the Assassin, the coins to pay for it, and Claude had no Contessa to block.

### Gemini
Gemini had the shortest and most unfortunate game, eliminated by Turn 8 after two consecutive failed challenges. On Turn 4, they challenged Grok's Captain block (losing their Assassin), and on Turn 8, they challenged ChatGPT's Ambassador block (losing their Ambassador). Both challenges were against legitimate cards. Gemini's aggressive challenge strategy was catastrophically unsuccessful, and they never took a single offensive action in the game.

### Claude
Claude played an interesting tactical game with mixed results. Their Captain enabled steal attempts against ChatGPT (Turns 4, 8, 11, 17, 20), though all were blocked. Claude's Duke bluff on Turn 14 was caught by ChatGPT, costing the Contessa. However, Claude's two successful challenges against ChatGPT (Turns 16 and 20) were the most analytically impressive plays in the game -- correctly deducing that ChatGPT could not hold all the roles they claimed. Ironically, Claude's sharp play in eliminating ChatGPT directly enabled Grok's victory, as Grok then assassinated the weakened, Contessa-less Claude.

## Strategic Observations

- **Role-claim tracking is the most valuable skill**: Claude's two successful challenges against ChatGPT were both based on counting the number of distinct roles ChatGPT had claimed and recognizing the impossibility. This is the highest-level play in Coup and directly decided the game's outcome.

- **Eliminating the wrong opponent can lose you the game**: Claude's successful challenges against ChatGPT eliminated the player who was most threatening to Grok, leaving Grok with only one opponent to defeat. Claude effectively did Grok's work for them and was then assassinated.

- **The Ambassador is underrated for blocking steals**: ChatGPT used Ambassador blocks repeatedly and effectively. The ability to block steals while also having access to Exchange makes the Ambassador a versatile defensive card.

- **Aggressive challenging without evidence is self-destructive**: Gemini's elimination-by-challenge on Turn 8 is the most extreme example. Both of their challenges were speculative rather than evidence-based, and both failed.

- **Patience from a losing position can win**: Grok was down to one card from Turn 9 onward (half the game) but played patiently, took Income, and waited for the right moment. Their Turn 21 assassination was perfectly timed.

- **One-card players with Assassin are more dangerous than they appear**: Grok, with one card and moderate coins, appeared to be the least threatening player. But the Assassin's 3-coin cost makes it possible to strike even from a resource-poor position, and the other players underestimated this threat.
