## Strategic Observations

- **This game was defined by Duke dominance**: Both finalists (Claude and ChatGPT) held Duke and used Tax as their primary income source. The players without Duke (Grok and Gemini) were eliminated. Tax provided 3 coins per turn compared to Income's 1, creating an insurmountable economic gap.
- **The game was the longest of the set at 30 turns** because the final 1v1 between two Duke holders devolved into a Tax-trading stalemate where both players needed to accumulate 7 coins between coups. Neither player had any way to disrupt the other's economy.
- **Foreign Aid blocking** was a key secondary use of Duke. Claude blocking Grok's Foreign Aid twice effectively eliminated Grok's only alternative income source, condemning them to 1-coin Income turns.
- **Contessa is the weakest card when assassination is absent**: Grok held two Contessas but no one attempted assassination in this game. Contessa only blocks assassination, making it situationally useless.
- **Passive play can win**: ChatGPT demonstrated that in a game where you hold the right card (Duke), simply collecting Tax every turn and letting others fight can be a viable winning strategy. ChatGPT took essentially no risks the entire game.
- **The "free rider" problem**: Claude eliminated two players while ChatGPT eliminated zero (before the final coup). ChatGPT benefited from Claude's aggression without bearing any of the cost. This is a known strategic tension in multiplayer games.
