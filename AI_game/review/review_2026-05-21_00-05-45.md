## Winner Analysis

ChatGPT won by playing an extremely patient, low-profile game. Early on, ChatGPT used Exchange to completely reshape their hand, returning their starting Ambassador and Captain for what turned out to be an Assassin and likely a Contessa. ChatGPT then spent the middle game almost exclusively taking Income (Turns 5, 11, 14, 17, 21, 23), staying under the radar while Claude and Grok engaged each other with Coups and Tax.

ChatGPT's key strategic insight was letting the two Duke-holders (Claude and Grok) exhaust each other. While Claude spent coins couping Grok twice, ChatGPT quietly accumulated resources. When ChatGPT's assassination attempt was blocked on Turn 19, they simply reverted to Income-gathering and eventually reached Coup range. The final Coup on Turn 27 was inevitable -- Claude had only a Contessa left and no way to generate coins faster than 3 per turn (via Tax), while ChatGPT needed only to reach 7 coins.

ChatGPT's weakness was the over-reliance on Income rather than using claimed roles aggressively, but in this particular game, the passive strategy worked because the other players eliminated each other.

## Strategic Observations

- **Gemini's challenge heuristic was fundamentally broken.** Using coin count as a basis for challenging Duke claims makes no sense in Coup -- the Duke is most commonly claimed early precisely because it generates coins. Gemini applied this flawed heuristic twice and was eliminated by Turn 3.
- **The Tax/Duke meta dominated.** Three of four players opened with Tax claims (Claude, Grok, and later ChatGPT with Exchange). The Duke was the most valuable early-game card, and no one successfully challenged a legitimate Duke claim.
- **Passive play can win when opponents fight.** ChatGPT's Income-heavy strategy was viable only because Claude and Grok spent resources eliminating each other. In a game where ChatGPT was targeted, this strategy would have failed.
- **Claude's target selection was suboptimal.** Both of Claude's Coups targeted Grok, the player who was also generating 3 coins/turn via Tax. Meanwhile, ChatGPT was left unmolested to slowly build toward Coup range. A more balanced threat assessment might have changed the outcome.
- **The endgame devolved into a coin race.** After Grok's elimination, the Claude vs. ChatGPT duel was decided purely by who could reach 7 coins first, with no bluffing or interaction. This is a common weakness in two-player Coup endgames.
