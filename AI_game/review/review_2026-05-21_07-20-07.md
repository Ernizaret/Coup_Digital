- **Gemini's challenge-addiction is a consistent liability.** Across games, Gemini challenges early and often without evidence, losing cards rapidly. In this game, two failed challenges in six turns eliminated Gemini. This suggests Gemini's AI model has an overly aggressive challenge threshold.
- **Passive income-only strategies are non-viable.** Grok's 8 turns of income (8 coins) versus Claude's Tax and Steal income (easily 15+ coins over the same period) illustrates the massive economic gap. Players who do not claim role-based actions fall behind irreversibly.
- **Exchange information leakage can be exploited.** Claude's catch of ChatGPT's Captain bluff (Turn 15) was based entirely on remembering that ChatGPT returned a Captain during a previous exchange. - **Blocking steals with Duke (Foreign Aid) extends dominance.** Claude blocked Grok's Foreign Aid on Turn 20 with Duke, demonstrating that Duke holders can suppress opponents' economic alternatives, further widening the gap.
